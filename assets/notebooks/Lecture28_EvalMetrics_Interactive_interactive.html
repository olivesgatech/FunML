BLEU & ROUGE Scores

BLEU-n: n-gram precision in candidate vs reference, with brevity penalty (BP).
BLEU-n = BP · exp((1/n) Σk=1..n log pk) where pk = (matched k-grams) / (total candidate k-grams), BP = min(1, exp(1 − r/c)).
ROUGE-n: recall version — matched k-grams / total reference k-grams.
ROUGE-L: based on Longest Common Subsequence (LCS) length.

Formula

PPL(W) = exp(−(1/N) Σt log p(ct|ct−1))
Lower PPL = model finds the text more predictable. Real LLMs use much larger context (thousands of tokens) and vocabularies (50k+ tokens), but the formula is identical.

Bigram probability heatmap (top-16 chars)

Each cell = p(next char | prev char) estimated by counting from a small English training corpus (about 1 KB of text hardcoded in the page). Dark = high probability.

Per-character log-prob (candidate)

Sharp drops indicate surprising characters (low p under the model). The mean of −log p gives the cross-entropy; PPL = exp(mean −log p).

Levenshtein DP matrix (character-level)

Cell (i,j) holds the minimum edit cost to transform the first i chars of the reference into the first j chars of the candidate. Operations: insert, delete, substitute (cost 1 each). The white path traces the optimal alignment via backtrace.
